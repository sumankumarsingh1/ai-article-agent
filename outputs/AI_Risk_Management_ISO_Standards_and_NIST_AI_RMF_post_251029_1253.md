**AI Risk Management: ISO Standards and NIST AI RMF**

    The rapid advancement of AI technologies has brought unprecedented opportunities, but also significant risks. To ensure responsible AI development and deployment, organizations must implement robust risk management frameworks. Two key frameworks gaining traction are the **ISO/IEC 23894:2023** standard and the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF)**.

    **ISO/IEC 23894:2023** provides a structured approach to AI risk management, emphasizing risk identification, assessment, and mitigation. It aligns with broader risk management principles, ensuring AI systems are developed and deployed in a way that minimizes harm. The standard is particularly valuable for organizations seeking a globally recognized framework to guide their AI risk management efforts.

    The **NIST AI RMF**, on the other hand, offers a flexible, voluntary framework designed to help organizations manage AI-related risks. It focuses on four core functions: **Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage**. This framework is adaptable to different industries and AI applications, making it a practical tool for organizations at various stages of AI adoption.

    Both frameworks highlight the importance of transparency, accountability, and continuous monitoring in AI risk management. As AI continues to evolve, adherence to these standards will be crucial for building trust and ensuring ethical AI deployment.

    **Key Takeaways:**
    - AI risk management is essential for responsible AI development.
    - ISO/IEC 23894:2023 and NIST AI RMF provide structured approaches to managing AI risks.
    - Transparency, accountability, and continuous monitoring are critical.
    - Organizations should align with these frameworks to build trust and ensure ethical AI deployment.
